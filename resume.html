This significantly reduced false escalations, improved operational focus, and enabled engineering teams to prioritize incidents based on evidence rather than assumptions.

The resulting platform accelerated root-cause analysis, improved decision quality during incidents, and enabled evidence-based operational reporting on demand.

Developed a forensic search capability that reconstructed complete user journeys by correlating application events, latency, failures, and behavioural baselines (P95/P99), enabling explainable incident analysis and faster root-cause identification.

Designed statistical modelling pipelines using scikit-learn and NeuralProphet to establish historical traffic baselines, seasonal trends, and expected system behaviour. Compared live telemetry against learned baselines to identify meaningful deviations and eliminate dependence on static alert thresholds.

Implemented real-time behavioural clustering to differentiate normal activity from anomalous behaviour, enabling incidents to be prioritised by behavioural deviation and business impact rather than alert volume. Improved signal quality, reduced false positives, and enabled earlier detection of emerging operational issues.
